♔ ChessMaine ♔

Serving Players and Hosts Since 1974

ChessMaine is Maine's premier chess organization, hosting up to 50 tournaments for 750 players annually.

Our Story

In 1971, Maine chess great Roger Morin founded ChessMaine to promote and encourage chess activity in Maine. Morin aimed to deepen community relations, foster intellectual growth, and sustain a tradition of competition. Now a subset of the Maine Chess Association (MECA), ChessMaine has hosted over 2,000 tournaments since its inception, serving players from over 45 states and 20 nations.

Tournaments This Week

ChessMaine works hard to promote and encourage chess activity in Maine, hosting weekly rated tournaments near renowned tourist destinations from Kittery to Fort Kent and everywhere in between. Tournaments are open to everyone, from chess veterans to those just learning how to move the pieces. All supplies, including boards and clocks, are provided. Entry fees are modest and directly support the Maine Chess Association.

ChessMaine in the News

The Story of MECA's International Matches

Maine Chess Association President Michael Dudley recaps the fascinating story of MECA's three international chess matches over the past three months. Since December 2020, MECA has faced off against the Republic of Tuva, a remote Russian republic in Southern Siberia; Bikin, a town in the far-eastern Khabarovsk Krai Region of Russia; and the Tromsø Chess Club in Norway.

Get Involved

MECA is always looking for passionate chess players to host tournaments. To host, you must be over 18, be familiar with the US Chess Federation's rules and regulations, and have an active USCF membership. Being a host qualifies you for MECA-exclusive tournaments and discounts on entry fees! You can apply below immediately, or contact Michael Dudley for more information.
